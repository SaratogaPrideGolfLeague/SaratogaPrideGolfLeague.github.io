Rules and Regulation to Play by

The following set of rules and regulations govern our league, ensuring a fair and equitable playing environment for all participants. These guidelines not only establish our scoring system but also determine tee-off times for league members.

  • Our league implements a modified Handicap (HCP) scoring system that takes into account adjusted scoring for a fair and balanced competition.
  • Following the adjusted scoring, our scoring system assigns points as follows: double bogey or worse = 0 points, bogey = +1, par = +2, birdie = +4, eagle = +8, albatross = +16.
  • Mulligan shots are not permitted; each shot must be played as it lies.
  • In the event of an unplayable lie, you may drop the ball within two club lengths of the original spot, choosing a more favorable lie.
  • Dropping the ball away from an unplayable or unfavorable lie incurs no stroke penalty.
  • If you exceed 10 strokes on a hole, kindly pick up your ball, mark your score sheet with a 10, and proceed to the next hole.
  • Once you start your round from a specific tee color (red, gold, white, blue, or black), it is required to play all subsequent holes from that tee box.
  • There are no "give me" shots on the green; for a score to count, the ball must be holed in the cup on each hole.
  • Enjoyment and socializing are encouraged during the round, including moderate drinking. However, should any behavioral issues related to excessive drinking be reported to the league, a warning will be issued. Continuation of such behavior may result in the revocation of league membership.
  • Above all, remember to have fun and show respect to all individuals on the course, whether league members or not. We are all united in our pursuit of enjoying a round of golf.
